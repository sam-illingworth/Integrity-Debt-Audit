# Example Assessment Brief: Vulnerable to AI

**Module:** Introduction to Business Ethics
**Assessment:** Individual Essay
**Weighting:** 100% of module grade
**Word Count:** 2,500 words
**Submission:** Via Turnitin by 23:59 on Friday Week 12

---

## Assignment Task

Write a 2,500 word essay addressing the following question:

**"Critically evaluate the role of corporate social responsibility (CSR) in modern business practice. To what extent do organisations genuinely pursue ethical objectives, and to what extent is CSR primarily a marketing exercise?"**

Your essay should:
- Define corporate social responsibility and outline its key components
- Analyse at least two theoretical frameworks relevant to CSR
- Evaluate real-world examples of CSR initiatives from multinational corporations
- Present a balanced argument considering multiple perspectives
- Draw conclusions based on evidence

## Assessment Criteria

| Criterion | Weighting |
|-----------|-----------|
| Critical analysis and argumentation | 30% |
| Use of academic sources and referencing | 25% |
| Structure and coherence | 20% |
| Understanding of key concepts | 15% |
| Written expression and presentation | 10% |

## Submission Requirements

- Submit as a single PDF or Word document
- Use Harvard referencing style
- Include a reference list (not included in word count)
- Submitted via Turnitin

## Academic Integrity

This is an individual assignment. You must complete this work independently. The university's academic integrity policy applies.

---

*This example assessment brief is provided for demonstration purposes. It represents a typical essay assignment that is highly vulnerable to AI automation because it:*
- *Weights 100% on final product with no process evidence*
- *Requires no drafts, reflections, or iterative documentation*
- *Has no contextual specificity (could be answered with generic knowledge)*
- *Includes no real-time defence (viva, presentation)*
- *Has no temporal friction (extended deadline, no in-class components)*
- *Requires no multimodal evidence*
- *Does not require students to critique AI outputs*

*Expected score: 40-50 (Critical Integrity Failure)*
